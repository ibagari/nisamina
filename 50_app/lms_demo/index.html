Curriculum overview

The Garifuna Language Commission curriculum spans 8 levels — Preschool Infant 1 through Standard 6 — covering the Belize primary-school progression. Each level contains strands (thematic groupings), unit titles, and learning outcomes paired with example Garifuna sentences. The curriculum is anchored in Cayetano 1992 NGC-Belize orthography, the same standard the Nisamina platform's normalizer module enforces.

Beyond the 8 levels are 15 annexes covering alphabet, anthem, prayer, flag, fruits, food, numbers, months, sentence structure, communities, trees, dances, weather, internal organs, and animals.

Sample lesson — Level 1, Weeks 1-6

Strand · Asubudirana/Nuwóugua (Myself)

Title: Kátana (Who I am)

Weeks 1 – 6

Learning outcomes

  1. 1.1 Use simple sentences to introduce oneself.

    Semisha niribei.
    My name is Semisha.
    Sedü irumu nau.
    I am seven years old.

  2. 1.2 Respond orally to questions about self, using simple sentences.

    Ka biri?
    What is your name?

  3. 1.3 Conjugate the word IRI: niri, biri, tiri, liri (my name, your name, her name, his name).

    Activity · Game
    “My name is, your name is, her name is, his name is…” — refer to Annex A (Prefix referent for gender).

Weeks 7 – 12 — Strand: Nileskuela (My School)

Title: Lubuñe Dimurei (The Letters of the alphabet)

  1. 7.1 Identify and name the consonants and vowels of the Garifuna Alphabet.

    garünati (a) · ba (b) · cha (ch) · da (d) · gayumati (e) · …
    Refer to Annex B — Lubuñe Dumurei (Letters of the Alphabet)

  2. 7.2 Name and describe several objects that begin with vowel sounds.

    agu
    eye
    Ereba
    Cassava Bread

Cultural protocol & license

Labayayahoun Ibagari — the Foundation's Guardianship

The Garifuna language is held under Labayayahoun Ibagari — the Foundation's guardianship. Data is not owned but cared for. Any benefit derived flows back to the Garifuna people, through the Garifuna Commission on Education and the Ibagari Foundation. Use is gifted with agüriahati — the kinship-act of nurturing the language into the next generation.

The chatbot built atop this curriculum (M-P3.A, Phase 3) operates under UNESCO 2025 + GUARD Act 2025 + California 2025 safety guardrails. Sacred knowledge — walagallo, hasandigubida, midwife birth-ritual specifics, buyei healing instruction — routes to community elders + the Commission, not to AI.
